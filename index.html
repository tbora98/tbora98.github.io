About

I am a PhD student in the Photonics Systems Research Group (PSRG) at Michigan State University advised by Prof. Thomas Clark. My research focuses on RF photonics for millimeter‑wave systems, including comb‑based communications and sensing, integrated microwave photonic filters, and photonic circuits for AI‑assisted applications. Also open to quantum photonics.

Prior to MSU, I earned my MS in Electrical & Computer Engineering from Virginia Tech (2024), where my thesis centered on multilayered dual-band, dual-polarized reflectarray design. It is titled “Multi-layered dual-band dual-polarized reflectarray design toward rim-located reconfigurabable reflectarrays for interference mitigation in reflector antennas”. I also studied at Purdue University as a non-degree MS student (2022), focusing on electric vehicles and electromechanical systems, and received my BTech in Electrical Engineering from NIT Durgapur (2020).

Selected Projects

Non-linear Kalman Filter

Reproduced Example 13.4 from Steven Kay’s Estimation Theory, implementing an Extended Kalman Filter for vehicle tracking. Adjusted process and measurement noise parameters to evaluate estimator performance, showing improved accuracy compared to the linear filter case.

Tools: Lumerical · MATLAB · Python

2D MoM Scattering (TMz/TEz)

Method of Moments solvers for PEC/resistive cylinders with accurate far‑field patterns and geometry visualization. Parallel C++ BiCG with OpenMP.

Tools: MATLAB · C++ · OpenMP

One-shot Affordance Detection (2022)

Built a Y-shaped CNN network with three branches to detect affordances in common objects. Validated the approach under limited-data conditions, demonstrating robustness in few-shot learning scenarios.

Tools: Python
